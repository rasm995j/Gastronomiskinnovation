Buffalo worms crunch for breakfest

  • 150g Buffallo worms
  • 50g brown sugar
  • 1/2dl water

Pour the brown sugar on a pan. Pour the water ontop and let it melt. When the brown sugar has melted, put the buffalo worms in and mix it all. Keep mixing till the worms are dry.
you may add any kind of breakfast topping you want

Meatballs with wax larvae

  • 2tsp. rapeseed oil
  • 150g wax larvae(frozen)
  • 1 chopped onion
  • 2 cloves of garlic
  • 1 tsp. paprika
  • 1/2kg peeled potatoes, cut in 4
  • 1/4 of a Hokkaido pumpkin, cut in half
  • 2 eggs
  • 100g grated cheddar
  • 1dl rasp of good quality
  • 1tsp. salt

Boil the potatoes and pumpkin till they are tender. while they are boiling, fry the onions and garlic on a pan with rapeseed oil. Add the wax larvae and and fry them for a minute. Add some paprika and salt to the wax larvae. stir it all for a couple of minutes. Now add it all to a bowl. Also add the hot potatoes, pumpkins, rasp and salt. Stir it all together, but not to much. Split the eggs into white and yellow. Mix he white solid and turn them in the mixture. Let then breath for 1/3 hour. form balls. Roll them on a plate with some rapeseed oil and then a bowl with rasp. Now add them to a baking tray with baking paper and bake them for 15 minutes in a 200 degree preheated ovn.

Cookies with buffalo worms

  • 150g Buffalo worms
  • 250g soft butter
  • 250g brown sugar
  • 1 egg
  • 5dl wheat flour
  • 80g chopped walnuts
  • 200g chopped dark chocolate

Mix the butter and brown sugar in a large bowl. When is it bended together then add egg. Blend it till the mixture is smooth, then add the flour little by little, while you mix. Afterwards addthe chopped dark chocolate, walnuts and buffalo worms.

Form the mixture to 18 balls and place them on a oven tray with baking paper. Make sure there is space in between the balls. flatten the balls till they are cookies with approximately 1cm high. Bake them in a preheaded oven at 175 degree. for 8-10 minutes. The cookies should be a little dry in the middle.
